About Me

I am a senior computer engineering student who plans to pursue a PhD in robotics upon graduating. My passions include robotics, automatic control systems, cyber-physical systems, signal processing, and integrated circuits. My aim is to use mathematical and computational methods to improve mass public transit and entertainment control systemsâ€”from autonomous trains to skyscraping rollercoasters. These are complex machines that need robust controllers to maintain stability and safety.

My passion for engineering design led me to pursue computer engineering at the University of Maryland-Baltimore County. I began computer vision and robotics research in my sophomore year, which only furthered my interest in complex mechanical systems. One of my biggest turning points was taking circuit theory and signals and systems, which opened my eyes to a whole new application of differential equations capable of modeling the complex systems I was fascinated with.

Another major aspect about me is my love of art, education, and presentations. I presented at a conference for the first time last summer as a lightning round speaker at the Summer Undergraduate Research Fest (SURF) at UMBC. I was able to combine my artistic drawing skills with storytelling to explain my robotics and computer vision research at a high level to those outside of my field. It was so moving to see so many people appreciate my artwork and genuinely grasp these complex concepts without any technological background.

I hope to continue to share my research findings with people all around the world and find creative ways to incorporate my artistic skills wherever possible. My long-term goal is to utilize robotics and control theory to develop human-centered, reliable systems for applications in transportation infrastructure and entertainment.
